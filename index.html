What is Phishing?

Deceptive Attempt

Fraudulent attempt to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.

Common Channels

Emails, text messages, social media, or phone calls that appear legitimate.

Main Goals

Steal login credentials, install malware, or gain access to sensitive data.

How to Recognize Phishing

Urgent or threatening language

Suspicious sender's email address

Hover to check URLs before clicking

Poor spelling and grammar

Real-world Examples

Fake Bank Alert

"Your account has been locked. Click here to verify your information."

Banks never ask for sensitive information via email

Unexpected Prize

"You've won a prize! Click here to claim your reward."

If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is

You receive an email from your bank asking you to confirm your account details by clicking a link. What should you do?

Best Practices

Verify the Sender

Always check the sender's email address carefully.

Use Multi-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system and applications.

When in Doubt, Ask

Verify suspicious messages with your IT department.
